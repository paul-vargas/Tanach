PDF files

The proprietary Portable Document Format (PDF) provides full-featured, cross-platform representation of paged documents. PDF files can be viewed and printed with the free  Adobe Reader. PDF files are available only in a fixed format. Users needing a different format should obtain an ODT file and then convert it to PDF via the OpenOffice Writer "Export to PDF" button.

PDF files for entire biblical books can be obtained by clicking on the book name on the Home page. The middle row of the resulting page offers a table of available formats and contents. In the row labelled "PDF", click the link corresponding to the desired content ("Accents", "Vowels", "Consonants","Morphology"). The files are stored in the PDFFiles subdirectory.

Zipped archives of all Tanach books in PDF with content xxx (where xxx is one of "acc", "vow". "con", or "mor"), Tanach.xxx.pdf.zip, are also available in the PDFFiles subdirectory. These files are obtained from the book ODT files via OpenOffice Writer on a Windows 7 operating system.

Printing on both sides of the paper.

Although the PDF files are free they're an ecologically dubious and uneconomic way to obtain large amounts of printed Tanach text.
Adobe Reader 9.3.4 can be set to print odd and even pages separately. This allows two-sided printing with simple printers. Here's how:
  1. Activate the "Print" window in Acrobat Reader. The "All" radio button should be set by default.
  2. In the middle-left of the "Print" window locate the title and pulldown menu "Subset: All Pages in Range". Pull this down to set "Odd Pages Only".
  3. Press the "Print" button on the lower right side of the window.
  4. Reorder the printed odd pages so that the blank side of the first page will be printed first.
  5. Place the odd pages in the printer with the blank sides and the top of the pages feeding first into the printer.
  6. Activate the "Print" window in Acrobat Reader again.
  7. Set the "Subset: All Pages in Range" pulldown to set "Even Pages Only".
  8. Press the "Print" button again.
  9. Reorder the pages according to the page numbers.
The book of Micah is a good test case. Staple the pages together on the right side.
